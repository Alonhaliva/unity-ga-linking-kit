GA Linking — Cheat Sheet

Info I gathered from Slack channels, client questions, and from Tatiana Hoffman and Tatiana Ventsova.
The short pitch

“Linking your Google Analytics takes under a minute and uses data you’re already collecting. It just gives our system more to work with so it makes better calls on which users and placements are worth more. Easy win, basically no effort on your end.”

When clients push back

No match — try another word.

Setup — what the client needs

The person doing the linking needs all three

  • Admin on the GA property
  • Owner or Admin on GCP (IAM)
  • BigQuery Data Viewer

Also: be on the latest Unity Ads SDK / Bold. And tell them to screen-record it — makes fixing any hiccup way easier.

If something breaks

  • Linked but no data showing? Project Settings → Developer Data → next to “Service Account BigQuery Access” → Run Auto-fix. Heads up: the fix isn’t retroactive, so they have to re-run it.
  • Make sure the DDF toggle (“Unity Ads Performance”) is on — needs an Org Owner.
  • Don’t freak out about delays. Reporting runs ~48h behind, refreshes around 11:45 PT daily. New links take a couple days to show in the sheet/Looker.
Auto-fix didn’t do the trick? There’s a manual route. The Manual BigQuery Link Fix Guide walks the client through the exact steps by hand — granting the Unity service account GA Editor + BigQuery Data Viewer access and checking the export settings. Good to keep in your back pocket for trickier clients.

Good questions to ask before pushing Tatiana H’s idea

  • How useful is GA data to you today?
  • How does it fit with the other signals you use?
  • What data do you lean on most, and why?

Tip from Tatiana V (for clients who’ve already connected but hit a debug / data-flow issue): with the tricky/sensitive ones, wait till the fix is fully confirmed before re-approaching. With the easy ones, just go.
